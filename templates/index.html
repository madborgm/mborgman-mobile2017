My "Quantified Self" Experiment

From three weeks, I ran the AWARE framework on my iPhone to track some of my daily activities: locations, means of transportation, etc. The application did not track more qualitative data such as mood, type of activities, and reasoning behind locations. In order to make up for this qualitative gap, I looked through all of my data (over 40,000 instances) to try and find interesting patterns.

Below are a few tables representing some of my insights.

For these tables, I converted the UNIX EPOCH time stamp to a data and time format in Wrangler. I couldn't find a script to change location from Latitude and Longitude, therefore, I located the specific locations through Google Maps and coded them as a "layman" location that I remembered from my walk to class.

Where Was I?

I'm a creature of habit. I wake up every weekday at the same time (6:00 AM). I walk the same path everyday to class/campus. Instead of looking for some abnormalities in my data, I wanted to determine if I was as habitual as I thought. After looking through my location data, I found that I was most frequently tracked to the same four places.

The New York Outlier

While I was looking through my data, I was trying to decide if there was a pattern between the location label and areas. I tested three latitudes/longitudes with the label 37 and found that they were all located near my apartment. I decided that the "37" label must be my apartment. However, when I was inputting locations to a map, some of the latitudes and longitudes correlated to New York. I thought there must have been an error in my data, until I remembered that I had recently traveled to New York.

"Track"-ing

As I was examining how habitual my daily routine was, I began to wonder if I could use this assumption to compare how fast I could walk to class. I combed through my data to find my locations and times during times I knew I walked from my apartment to class. I calculated my distance (it varied on certain days) and the total time it took from leaving my apartment to being in class. With these numbers, I could calculate my average speed. Additionally, I found data on the weather for each day to compare if that was a factor.
